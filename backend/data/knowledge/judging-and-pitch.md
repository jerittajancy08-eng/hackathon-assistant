# Judging Criteria and Pitch Preparation

Category: Judging Criteria
Source: internal-hackbot-guide

Common judging criteria:

- Innovation: the idea is novel, creative, or applies technology in a fresh way.
- Technical complexity: the team built meaningful engineering work, not only a static mockup.
- Impact: the project solves a real problem for a clear user group.
- Presentation: the team explains the problem, solution, demo, and value clearly.
- Feasibility: the project can realistically be continued after the hackathon.

How to improve scores:

- Connect the project to a specific user and problem.
- Demo the most important workflow, not every feature.
- Explain what was built during the hackathon.
- Mention technical challenges and how the team solved them.
- Show measurable impact or a realistic adoption path.

Pitch deck structure:

- Slide 1: project name, tagline, team.
- Slide 2: problem and target user.
- Slide 3: solution overview.
- Slide 4: live demo or screenshots.
- Slide 5: technical architecture.
- Slide 6: impact, market, or user value.
- Slide 7: challenges, learnings, and future scope.
- Slide 8: ask, closing, or thank you.

Demo tips:

- Use a stable demo path.
- Prepare seed data before presenting.
- Keep backup screenshots or a short demo video.
- Assign one person to drive the demo and one person to narrate.
- Avoid logging into services live unless necessary.

Judge Q&A preparation:

- Be ready to explain why the problem matters.
- Know what is real, mocked, or planned.
- Explain your stack choices.
- Mention privacy, security, and scalability when relevant.
- Answer honestly and briefly, then return to impact.
