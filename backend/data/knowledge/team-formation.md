# Team Formation

Category: Team Formation
Source: internal-hackbot-guide

Good hackathon teams usually have 3 to 5 members. A solo project can work for small events, but a balanced team usually moves faster because design, backend, frontend, pitching, and testing happen in parallel.

Recommended team roles:

- Product lead: defines the problem, user, scope, and final story.
- Frontend developer: builds the user interface, user flows, responsiveness, and demo polish.
- Backend developer: builds APIs, database models, integrations, authentication, and deployment.
- AI or domain specialist: handles model usage, data, prompts, evaluation, or subject-matter logic.
- Designer or presenter: improves UX, slides, demo script, storytelling, and judge Q&A.

Responsibilities:

- Decide the problem statement in the first 10 to 20 percent of the event.
- Keep the MVP small and demoable.
- Assign one owner for the final pitch.
- Maintain a shared task board.
- Freeze core scope before the final few hours.
- Prepare fallback screenshots or a local demo video.

Team size guidance:

- 1 member: best for a focused prototype or learning project.
- 2 members: good for frontend plus backend or prototype plus pitch.
- 3 members: strong minimum for a serious hackathon submission.
- 4 members: ideal for product, frontend, backend, and AI/design split.
- 5 members: useful for larger builds, but coordination becomes more important.
